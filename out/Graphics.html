Normal vectors

Remember: for column vectors AA and BB, A⋅B=ATBA\cdot B=A^TB.

A normal vector NN will be perpendicular (by definition) to a tangent vector VV (so N⋅V=NTV=0N\cdot V=N^TV=0).
When VV is transformed by MM to become MVMV, NN must be transformed by a different transformation GG such that GNGN is perpendicular to MVMV, so (GN)⋅(MV)=(GN)TMV=0\left(GN\right)\cdot\left(MV\right)=\left(GN\right)^TMV=0.
Hence, as (AB)T=BTAT(AB)^T=B^TA^T, NTGTMV=0N^TG^TMV=0. But from before, we also have NTV=0N^TV=0.
Therefore, NTGTMV=NTVGTM=IG=(M−1)T N^TG^TMV=N^TV\\ G^TM=I\\ G=(M^{-1})^T
